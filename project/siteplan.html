Kabianga Student Housing Guide

Website Planning Document

Site Name

The site name is Kabianga Student Housing Guide. This name was chosen because it clearly identifies the target audience (students) and the location (Kabianga). The name is simple, professional, and easy to remember, making it suitable for both students searching for housing and landlords offering accommodation.

Optional domain availability: kabiangastudenthousing.com

Site Purpose

The purpose of this website is to help students around Kabianga find suitable off-campus housing by providing clear and organized information about available student apartments. The site will display details such as rent ranges, room types, water availability, distance from campus, and general housing conditions.

The website will also allow students to interact with the content by viewing apartment availability, saving favorite apartments, estimating rent costs, and submitting housing inquiries. Each apartment listed on the site is managed by an individual landlord, making the platform useful for connecting students with apartment owners.

Scenarios

  • What student apartments are available near Kabianga, and how much do they cost per month?
  • How far is a specific apartment from campus, and does it have reliable water supply and suitable room options?
  • How can I contact a landlord or send a housing inquiry for an apartment I am interested in?

Color Schema

The color scheme for the website is designed to be simple, clean, and student-friendly, while remaining professional for landlords.

  • Dark Blue (#1f3a5f): Used for headings, navigation bar, and important highlights to create a professional and trustworthy feel.
  • Light Gray (#f4f4f4): Used for page backgrounds and content sections to improve readability and reduce eye strain.
  • Accent Green (#2ecc71): Used for buttons, links, and call-to-action elements such as “View Apartments” or “Send Inquiry.”

Typography

The website will use clean and readable fonts to ensure good usability for students accessing the site on both mobile and desktop devices.

  • Roboto: Used for body text and paragraphs because it is easy to read and works well on screens of all sizes.
  • Montserrat: Used for headings and navigation items to create clear visual hierarchy and emphasis.

Wireframes

The wireframes below describe the general layout of the home page for both mobile and desktop views. The design follows a simple and responsive structure to ensure usability on different screen sizes.

Mobile View

In the mobile view, the layout will be displayed in a single column. The navigation menu will appear at the top, followed by a brief introduction section. Apartment listings will be stacked vertically, with buttons for viewing details or saving favorites. The footer will appear at the bottom of the page.

Desktop View

In the desktop view, the navigation menu will be displayed horizontally at the top of the page. The main content area will use a wider layout, with apartment listings displayed in a grid format. Additional information such as filters or tips may appear beside the main content. The footer will remain at the bottom of the page.
